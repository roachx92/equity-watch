## Executive summary — investment thesis

IREN is a Sydney-headquartered, NASDAQ-listed builder-owner of renewable-powered data centers — ~160MW of British Columbia hydro sites, a 750MW campus at Childress, Texas, and an energized ~1.4GW first phase (April 2026) of a ~2GW hub at Sweetwater, West Texas — that spent 2024–25 as one of the largest listed Bitcoin miners (~50 EH/s installed at 6/30/25 [P]) and is now executing a deliberate, near-total pivot into AI compute. The pivot is structurally different from its peer cohort's: where Cipher, TeraWulf, Hut 8, Applied Digital and CleanSpark became **landlords** (10–25-year triple-net leases to investment-grade or IG-backstopped tenants), IREN chose to be an **operator** — it buys the GPUs (via ~$5.8B of Dell orders [P/T]), owns them, and sells GPU cloud services. Its two anchor contracts are cloud-services deals: **$9.7B / 5 years with Microsoft** (signed 2025-11-02, GB300-class GPUs in four tranches at Childress, 20% prepaid per tranche [P]) and **$3.4B / 5 years with NVIDIA itself** (signed 2026-05-07, for NVIDIA's internal workloads [P]). The debate on this name is not quality of counterparty — Microsoft is AAA, NVIDIA is AA — it is **conversion**: as of 3/31/26, **zero Microsoft tranches had been delivered or accepted, so $0 of the $9.7B sits in RPO** [P]; total company RPO was $710.3M [P]; recognized AI Cloud revenue was $33.6M for the quarter [P]. The market spent June–July repricing exactly that gap — the stock has halved from ~$66 (early June) to $33.62 (Jul 17) amid a sector-wide chip rout, a "neocloud unwind," and a Bernstein critique of IREN's customer concentration beyond Microsoft [T].

The three hard facts that anchor the snapshot: **(1)** Q3 FY26 (Mar-qtr) revenue was $144.8M — still 77% Bitcoin mining ($111.2M) vs. $33.6M AI Cloud — with a net loss of $(247.8)M including $140.4M of miner-decommissioning impairments, and management disclosed it will **cease all Bitcoin mining at Childress over time** with ~$520M of further impairments estimated [P]. **(2)** The company closed a **$3.65B investment-grade GPU financing** (Fitch A / DBRS A-low; ~$2.10B US private placement + $1.55B delayed-draw term loan, blended ~6.0% coupon) on 2026-06-01, secured against the GPUs and the Microsoft contracted cash flows — funding ~96% of the Microsoft-related GPU capex alongside the ~$1.9B prepayment [P/T]. **(3)** The share count is up +38.5% YoY to 357.4M, a $6.0B ATM (filed 2026-03-04) has ~$5.0B remaining, $3.75B of converts are outstanding (strikes $13.64–$85.63), and NVIDIA holds rights to 30M shares at $70 [P].

**Three reasons to own, each with its honest counterweight.** *First*, the power moat is real and scarce: ~2.9GW of energized/available owned power with freehold land, owned substations, and sub-$0.04/kWh renewable cost — the industry's binding constraint — at a cited ~$4M/MW build cost vs. ~$10M industry norms [T/T3]. *Counterweight:* the moat is in the power layer, but the equity story is levered to the **cloud service layer bolted on top, which is commoditizing** — CoreWeave, Nebius, Crusoe and the hyperscalers sell the same NVIDIA silicon, and GPUs depreciate in 3–4 years. *Second*, the contracted anchor is about as creditworthy as contracts get — $13.1B of TCV across AAA Microsoft and AA NVIDIA, 20% prepayments, and the capex ~96% financed at investment-grade cost of debt; deferred revenue has already jumped from $0.9M (6/30/25) to $120.4M (3/31/26) as prepayments land [P]. *Counterweight:* **contracted is not recognized** — $0 of the Microsoft TCV is in RPO until tranches are accepted, Horizon commissioning runs into early 2027, and the further ~$1.8B of the company's "$3.7B ARR by end-CY2026" target is explicitly **not contracted** [P/T2] — the exact slice the market is now refusing to pay for. *Third*, the reflexive discount: at ~$12B market cap the stock trades at roughly 3.2× its *targeted* ARR and ~4.3× *contracted* ARR — a fraction of neocloud comps — with 24.6% of the float sold short into a 50% drawdown; if delivery milestones simply land on schedule, the squeeze-and-re-rate path is mechanical [T]. *Counterweight:* short interest is high but days-to-cover is only ~1.8 (huge volume), so there is no structural squeeze lock; and the discount partly reflects genuine governance friction — co-founder super-voting B-class shares and an ~18.2M-share co-CEO RSU award reported ~July 1 [T, single-tier source; see §13].

**What has to be true:** (a) Microsoft's four GB300 tranches deliver and are *accepted* on schedule through 2026 → the $9.7B starts entering RPO and revenue; (b) the NVIDIA 60MW air-cooled ramp commissions early 2027; (c) the uncontracted ~$1.8B ARR slice finds counterparties without a distressed price cut; (d) capital markets stay open enough that the remaining Sweetwater build is funded by the ATM/project debt at tolerable dilution — while BTC mining, still 77% of revenue, is being deliberately shut down underneath the ramp; and (e) hyperscaler AI capex does not enter a digestion phase before (a)–(c) complete.

**Bottom line:** IREN owns arguably the best physical asset base in its cohort and has signed the best-credit contracts in the entire miner-pivot class — but it deliberately swapped the cohort's bond-like lease certainty for operator economics, and the market is currently pricing the swap as a mistake. The stock is a funded, contracted, high-beta construction story trading at a discount that resolves on delivery evidence, of which there is — as of today — one quarter's worth ($33.6M). The next two prints are the thesis.

## 1. Business model & role in the value chain

IREN develops, owns and operates its data centers end-to-end: it acquires freehold land, builds its own substations and high-voltage interconnections (compressing grid-queue timelines), contracts grid-connected renewable power (BC hydro; ERCOT wind/solar-heavy West Texas nodes), and monetizes the energized capacity through — historically — Bitcoin self-mining and — prospectively — AI Cloud services on owned NVIDIA fleets. FY2025: revenue $501.0M ($484.6M mining + $16.4M AI Cloud), net income $86.9M — the first profitable year after −$28.9M (FY24) and −$171.8M (FY23) [P]. Note the segment measure excludes D&A ($121.2M in Q3 alone [P]) — with 3–4-year GPU depreciation, the 86.3% AI Cloud "gross margin" is an ex-capital number; the real unit economics live below that line.

**What IREN is not:** it is not a landlord (no colocation segment exists yet — colocation is described in filings only as a potential future service [P]); it is not a chip maker; and after the announced Childress mining shutdown it will not primarily be a Bitcoin miner. **Position in the value chain:** it occupies two layers at once — the *power/land/shell* layer (where its cost advantage lives) and the *GPU cloud service* layer (where its revenue multiple lives). The AI Cloud segment is the equity story; mining is now transitional ballast being run down for its cash flow (~$75M/qtr OCF [P]) while the AI ramp is built. The May 2026 acquisitions — **Mirantis (~$625M, ~90% stock)** for orchestration software and **Nostrum Group (€165M)** [P] — move IREN up-stack toward full-service AI cloud, confirming the operator commitment.

## 4. Technological moat & competitive technologies

IREN's differentiation is not silicon — everyone rents the same NVIDIA parts — it is **energized power, owned end-to-end, at structurally low cost**, plus early liquid-cooling execution (Horizon 1 at Childress: purpose-built, direct-to-chip liquid-cooled, 50MW IT, energized Q4 2025 [T2/T]).

| Approach (Level 1) | How it works | Champions (2026) | Trade-off vs. IREN's approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Powered-shell colocation | Landlord delivers building+power; tenant installs cooling/compute (~$4–8M/MW) | APLD, CORZ, GLXY, traditional wholesale colo (QTS, Vantage) | Cheapest, lowest-risk for the landlord; thinnest value capture (rent only). IREN forgoes this certainty for cloud margin |
| Full turnkey DC leasing | Fully fitted, cooled hall under 10–25-yr triple-net lease (~$12–13M/MW) | WULF, HUT, CIFR, CLSK | Bond-like IG-backstopped cash flows the market currently rewards most; IREN's cloud ARR has contract terms but IREN keeps utilization risk |
| GPU-cloud / neocloud (IREN's model) | Operator owns GPUs, sells compute; highest revenue/MW, highest capex + depreciation + fill risk | **CoreWeave, Nebius, Crusoe, Fluidstack — and IREN** | Wins on margin capture; loses on capital intensity ($5.8B GPU orders) and 3–4-yr depreciation clocks |
| Behind-the-meter gas | On-site turbines bypass grid queues | Chevron+GE Vernova, ExxonMobil, Crusoe (historically) | Fastest speed-to-power but fuel/carbon-exposed; can't match IREN's sub-$0.04/kWh renewable cost |
| Co-located nuclear / SMR | DC sited at reactor; SMRs 2028+ | Talen, Constellation, hyperscaler SMR bets, Oklo | Best long-run clean baseload but NRC/timeline risk; IREN's power is live now |
| Grid-connected renewables (IREN core) | Owned land, substations, transmission rights; BC hydro + ERCOT | **IREN**, increasingly the cohort | Lowest all-in cost, 100% renewable; exposed to ERCOT interconnection/curtailment policy |
| Liquid cooling | Direct-to-chip/immersion, mandatory for GB300-class density | All serious players | Table stakes, not a moat — but IREN is credibly on the curve (Horizon 1 live), while legacy air-cooled mining shells face a retrofit capex tax |

**Convergence read:** the industry is converging on *energized grid-connected power with transmission rights* as the scarce input, while **bifurcating on monetization** — the market-rewarded model in mid-2026 is turnkey leasing to IG-backstopped tenants; IREN is the cohort's deliberate outlier on the operator side, cushioned by the only direct NVIDIA supplier-customer-shareholder relationship in the class. **Defensibility verdict: the power/land/interconnection layer is durable** (multi-year lead times, finite good sites, owned substations); **the cloud service layer is emerging optionality at best** — Mirantis-based orchestration is a catch-up integration play, not proprietary defensible IP; dynamic mining↔AI workload switching is clever capital efficiency, not a moat. Patents are not a factor in this thesis for any player [T — no material patent estate disclosed].

## 6. Secular / thematic positioning

IREN is **first-derivative** on the two scarcest inputs of the AI buildout — energized power and installed accelerators — and **second-derivative** on AI adoption itself (it sells capacity to those who sell intelligence). Within the theme it holds an unusual dual exposure: the *power scarcity* trade (its 2.9GW is the asset the entire sector is bidding for) and the *compute service* trade (GPU-hour economics). Its legacy BTC exposure is a third, unwanted, factor loading — WGMI's #3 holding at 10.33% (7/13) and DAPP at 5.94% [T] — that management is actively amputating via the Childress mining shutdown. The thesis-relevant secular question is not "will AI demand grow" but **which layer of the stack captures the growth**: if power stays the bottleneck, IREN's moat layer wins regardless of GPU-hour pricing; if accelerator supply loosens and capacity commoditizes, the operator layer's margins compress first. July 2026's tape — Meta reselling surplus compute, Micron/Intel/SK Hynix wobbles, the neocloud unwind — was the market stress-testing the second scenario.

## 8. Risks

**Pre-mortem — the named mechanism for a zero:** not BTC, not a hack — **a utilization break during a leveraged fleet build.** Specifically: hyperscaler AI capex enters digestion in 2027 just as IREN's uncontracted ~$1.8B ARR slice and post-tranche capacity come online; Microsoft — having prepaid only per-tranche — rephases later tranches and does not renew beyond year 5; GPU-hour pricing compresses under hyperscaler surplus-capacity resale (the Meta Compute mechanism); IREN's owned fleet — $5.8B+ of 3–4-year-life silicon financed at ~6% — strands, Adjusted EBITDA can't cover project-debt service after mining (deliberately shut down) no longer contributes, the ATM prints into a collapsing price until it can't, and the 2031–2033 converts ($3.3B, strikes $51.40–$85.63, hopelessly OTM in that world) become unrefinanceable hard debt. ## 10. Sector bottleneck / structural analysis

The binding constraint in AI infrastructure through at least 2028 is **energized, interconnected power** — not GPUs, not capital. Evidence: ERCOT's large-load queue exploded from 63GW (Dec 2024) to >438GW (~90% data centers) against a system peak record of 85.5GW [T]; transformer/HV-switchgear lead times gate every developer; hyperscalers are signing SMR/BTM deals for 2028+ capacity. IREN sits on the right side of the bottleneck — 2.9GW energized/available with owned substations and freehold land, acquired at mining-era prices — and its Sweetwater 1 energization (Apr 2026) added ~1.4GW at precisely the moment the queue made greenfield capacity a 3–5-year wait. The structural nuance: the bottleneck protects the *asset value* of IREN's power regardless of the cloud model's success (every landlord peer's lease signings re-mark it), but IREN monetizes the bottleneck only if it fills the capacity — the bottleneck does not guarantee *utilization* of an owner-operated fleet, only scarcity value of the megawatts. Batch Zero (PUCT, 6/18/26) cuts both ways: it disciplines the queue (good for incumbents with positions) while introducing curtailment-consent and feasibility gates (a new regulatory surface for a company 75%+ concentrated in ERCOT by capacity).
