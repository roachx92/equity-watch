**Dilution — the denominator is doing real work.** Weighted-average shares rose **+51.8% YoY** (50.0M → 76.0M), so a GAAP loss that grew **+56%** ($9.2M → $14.3M) produced an EPS that worsened by only **1¢**. Period-end shares 78,971K (from 74,998K at 12/31/25). Antidilutive overhang excluded from the loss-period denominator (10-Q Note 6): 2,886K convert shares + 2,240K RSUs + 1,324K vested Amazon-warrant shares = **6,450K**. A per-share figure improving on a swelling denominator is not an operating result.

**The Amazon-warrant correction — the common "warrant depressed revenue" read is wrong.** The 2025-03-13 Amazon warrant is booked as share-based consideration *capitalized as a contract asset*, amortized against revenue only as Amazon buys — **not** an upfront revenue hit. The $16.7M in the Q1'25 equity statement was capitalized (accumulated deficit moved only by the $9.2M net loss, confirming it). **Q1'26 warrant contra-revenue was only ~$0.8–1.0M**; $16.5M of the warrant asset remains capitalized. So Data Center's +154% is genuine, not a base artifact — **but $38.0M of Q1'26 revenue is Amazon-arrangement revenue, meaning Amazon now rivals Microsoft (28.8% FY25) as a DC anchor: a fresh single-customer concentration on the datacenter side to mirror Digicomm on CATV.**

**Contracted vs. recognized.** No numeric backlog is a filed disclosure — the 10-Q states contract durations <1 year, so RPO is "not applicable," and XBRL carries no contract-liability value. The ">$124M 800G / >$200M 1.6T" figures are **press-release/call-sourced, not filed** — and were *not* stated verbatim on the Q1'26 call (the $200M 1.6T order traces to the 2026-03-09 PR; the 800G framing to the Q4'25 call). Treat as management commentary.

A reader who stopped at "+51% revenue, adjusted EBITDA positive" got the quarter backwards.

### 2. What it means

#### (a) The underlying investment thesis

The deep-dive's conjunction: **AAOI converts the >$1.1B guide into recognized, margin-improving revenue (1.6T shipping on schedule) while the EML shortage holds the scarcity premium and Digicomm keeps paying through the DOCSIS wave — before dilution or a tailwind reversal catches up.**

- **Leg 1 — "convert the guide into recognized, margin-improving revenue": left largely untested, and what evidence exists cuts slightly negative.** The guide was *raised*, but it is verbal-only and back-half-weighted (Q3/Q4 each +60–80% QoQ), and the margin actually *fell* to 29.1%. 800G was $4.6M — the ramp is entirely ahead. Discounted by the **Guidance track record**: management hit its wide Q1 guide (at the low end, below Street) and has raised the FY once — a thin, one-quarter record on a number that isn't even filed.
- **Leg 2 — "EML shortage holds the scarcity premium": supported this quarter, with a dated expiry.** Management's "MOCVD on complete backlog" is corroborated by Nvidia's lock-up and peer commentary — but LightCounting's mid-2026 easing call means the premium is on a clock, and the relief accrues to Lumentum/Coherent.
- **Leg 3 — "Digicomm keeps paying through the DOCSIS wave": weakened on the margin.** Digicomm paid (no reserve), but its receivable *grew* +$47.7M to $222.7M / 74.5% of AR while CATV revenue was flat YoY — i.e. the balance-sheet exposure is rising faster than the revenue it supports, into a wave Dell'Oro sees cresting in 2026.

#### (b) Re-rating drivers — four buckets, kept separate

**Fundamental.** The print removed **no** discount and arguably added one: a Street miss, a *falling* gross margin against a pulled-forward 35% target, and a deteriorating cash cycle. The one positive — the raised FY guide — is unfiled and untested. **Timing unchanged** (1.6T still Q3–Q4 2026), but the burden of proof shifted entirely to H2.

**Structural.** The genuine positive: the EML-shortage narrative firmed (Nvidia lock-up, "complete backlog"), and peers' record prints validate the 800G→1.6T cycle. But this is a *sector* re-rate lever AAOI shares with COHR/LITE/FN, not an idiosyncratic one — and it is the lever LightCounting says starts easing mid-2026.

**Sentiment / technical.** **This bucket dominated the quarter and is where the honest reading lives.** The +42%/$233 move was half sector, squeeze-amplified (SI ~15%, DTC ~1.0), and **fully reversed to $102 within ~10 weeks**. The name is a β-3.69, IV-rank-~100 proxy for the optics tape — it re-rates up and down on flow, not fundamentals.

**Macro.** The US-onshoring angle firmed (Texas capacity, tariff hedge), a slow structural positive. Offsetting: the China-manufacturing base (57.5% of revenue) keeps tariff/export-control risk live.

**Net verdict — which bucket the call weighted most:** **sentiment/technical for the next 12 months** (the print's entire market verdict was a flow-driven round-trip, and the next catalyst is the same high-beta tape); **fundamental for the 3–5-year thesis** (only the H2 ramp + a real margin inflection can convert this into a franchise, and neither is proven). The fundamental bucket contributed *nothing* positive this quarter.

#### (c) Risks

- **Escalated — Digicomm concentration & working capital.** The single most important update: Digicomm AR **+$47.7M to $222.7M (74.5% of AR)**, ~87% of the QoQ receivable build, still **zero reserve**; the cash cycle at a 4-quarter-high 227 days. The exposure is growing faster than the revenue it finances. Dated, and moving the wrong way.
- **Newly surfaced — a second concentration.** **Amazon is now ~$38M/quarter (~25%) of revenue** on the datacenter side, mirroring Digicomm on CATV — the customer base is *two* deep concentrations now, not one.
- **De-escalated (modestly) — near-term financing access.** The $382M in-quarter ATM cleared at a healthy $104 WAP and a new $600M program is live — funding is available *today*. But see Tripwire #4: the $500M program was **exhausted in ~5 weeks**, and by 7/17 the share price ($102) is ~56% below the ATH, so the *next* raise is far more dilutive per dollar.
- **Pre-Mortem, updated.** The single most likely fundamental path to impairment remains a **Digicomm channel/credit event** — and the call moved that mechanism **closer**: the unreserved receivable is $47.7M larger, DSO re-extended, and the DOCSIS wave feeding Digicomm is nearer its 2026 crest. Nothing on the call addressed it; no analyst asked.
