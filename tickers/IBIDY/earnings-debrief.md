**Finding 1 — the ¥42bn FCF that isn't there.** The widely-quoted framing "OCF ¥106.4bn comfortably covers ¥64.3bn capex" **mixes a cash number with an accrual number.** Cash capex in the cash-flow statement was **¥106,118mn**. True FCF ≈ **+¥289mn**. The ~¥42bn gap reconciles cleanly: "accounts payable – other" fell ¥58,671 → ¥14,521 (**−¥44,150**) as Ibiden paid down prior-year capex accruals, and construction-in-progress fell ¥202,019 → ¥111,793 as Ono assets were placed in service. Nothing is being hidden — but **anyone modelling ~¥42bn of FCF is overstating cash generation by roughly that amount.** *(The ¥157.5bn financing outflow was funded by the cash pile plus ¥57.5bn of securities sales, not by FCF.)*

**Finding 2 — advances received are shrinking, not building.** Management themselves flagged in the interim report that OCF fell because advances "turned from increase to decrease" (前受金が増加から減少に転じた). If ¥500bn of customer pre-funding were arriving to underwrite the FY26–28 build — a plan approved in Feb 2026 — this line should be inflecting sharply **up**. It did the opposite.

**The contradiction worth naming:** FY3/2027 capex is guided to **¥210bn, more than triple** FY3/2026's ¥64.3bn accrual spend — against ~zero FCF, a shrinking advances line, and a cross-shareholding disposal pool that is now **81.2% exhausted** (the source of this year's ¥57.5bn of proceeds). The balance sheet is strong enough to absorb it (¥103bn net cash), but the funding runway is narrower than the "customers are paying for it" story implies.

### 2. What it means

#### (a) The underlying investment thesis

The deep-dive's conjunction: **the AI capex cycle keeps compounding · Ibiden fills its ¥500bn capacity at margin · organic substrate holds the high end until ~2028, after which Ibiden captures its share of the glass transition.**

- **Leg 1 — "the cycle is real and pricing is firm": SUPPORTED, and this is the strongest leg in the print.** Independently corroborated by the monopoly input supplier raising price ~30%, by three rivals being sold out, and by two other materials makers. Management put its own money behind it: the FY3/2028 OP target went **¥90bn → ¥150bn**.
- **Leg 2 — "capacity is pre-funded by customers": MATERIALLY WEAKENED, and this is the finding of the quarter.** No primary filing names a pre-funding customer or quantifies a prepayment; the ¥280/¥220 split is Ibiden's own plant capex; and **the advances line fell ¥11.1bn**, round-tripping to its Mar-2024 level. The de-risking that this leg supplies to the whole thesis is **not evidenced**. Note the sell-side is repeating it anyway (Nomura, 5/12).
- **Leg 3 — "fills at margin": UNTESTED.** FY3/2027 capex triples to ¥210bn against ~zero FCF. The first real read is the Q1 FY3/2027 print.
- **Leg 4 — "organic holds to ~2028, then Ibiden participates in glass": SUPPORTED and strengthened** — see §2(d), Tripwire #3.

**Discounted by the Guidance track record:** management sets conservative opening guides and raises (FY3/2026 OP guided ¥48.0bn → delivered ¥62.0bn). That argues the ¥90bn FY3/2027 OP guide is **a floor rather than a stretch** — a genuine positive. It does **not** extend to the mid-term ¥150bn target, which is two years out and rests on an un-auditable demand index.

#### (b) Re-rating drivers

**Fundamental.** Real and positive on the P&L: OP +45% guided, mid-term OP target +67%. **But the cash story is worse than the income statement** — FCF ~zero, capex tripling, advances reversing. Net: the fundamental bucket improved on earnings and *deteriorated* on cash quality, and the market appears to have noticed.

**Structural.** The genuinely strong bucket. The ABF super-cycle is corroborated chain-wide, and the **TSMC CoPoS disclosure (2026-06-11, after the print) names Ibiden** as build-up partner alongside Innolux, with HVM at **4Q28–1Q29** — i.e. the glass transition is arriving *later* than feared and *with* Ibiden inside it.

**Sentiment / technical.** **This bucket dominated the quarter and it is where the honest reading lives.** A +22% limit-up two days *before* the print, a +9% two-session pop, a complete round-trip in three sessions, and two months of flat price against a 45% guidance raise. The market is refusing to pay for these earnings.

**Macro.** Nikkei/JGB-yield risk-off drove the 5/15 give-back; **FX is an explicit ¥5.0bn contributor** to the FY3/2027 Electronics guide; Middle East risk is excluded from guidance by management's own statement.

**Net verdict:** **sentiment/technical carries the next 12 months** — the tape is not responding to fundamentals, and the multiple compressed through a large guidance raise. **Structural carries the 3–5 year thesis** — the ABF tightness and the CoPoS seat are the durable assets. The fundamental bucket is genuinely two-sided this quarter for the first time: strong earnings, weak cash.

#### (c) Risks

- **Escalated — quality of cash generation.** FCF ~zero on a cash basis; FY3/2027 capex guided to **¥210bn (3.3×)**; advances shrinking; the cross-shareholding disposal pool that funded this year's ¥57.5bn of proceeds is **81.2% exhausted**. The balance sheet (¥103bn net cash, 57.3% equity ratio) can absorb it — but the "self-funding, customer-financed" framing is not supported.
- **Escalated — the pre-funding premise itself.** Not a filing fact. Any model or thesis leaning on it should be re-underwritten deliberately.
- **Newly surfaced — CoWoP (chip-on-wafer-on-PCB).** Mounting AI chips **directly on the PCB, bypassing the package substrate entirely**, attacks the *category* rather than Ibiden's share within it. This is arguably a larger structural threat than the glass-core transition the thesis file is built around, and **it is not scoped in the deep-dive.** Flagged as under-researched — worth a dedicated pass.
- **Newly surfaced — Rubin Ultra content trim.** NVIDIA cancelled the 4-die Rubin Ultra package over **substrate warpage**, reverting to dual-die. Two-sided: it proves substrate physics is the industry bottleneck (thesis-supportive) but **a cancelled 4-die package is materially less substrate area per unit** than the prior roadmap implied — a direct trim to top-end content-per-unit.
- **Escalated — legacy erosion.** Philippines impaired two years running (~¥26bn cumulative) on PC-related demand management says it cannot foresee growing.
- **De-escalated — balance sheet and solvency.** Debt −44%, net cash rebuilt to ¥103bn, equity ratio 57.3%.
- **Quantified — dilution.** Convertible = **5.5%** (15,584k shares at ¥4,486.2), effectively live.

**Pre-mortem, updated.** The deep-dive's named mechanism — *glass-core substitution erodes Ibiden's value capture around 2028–2030* — **moved slightly further away** this quarter (CoPoS HVM 4Q28–1Q29 with Ibiden named as partner). But a **nearer-dated mechanism now sits alongside it**: capex tripling into ~zero free cash flow, with the customer-prepayment cushion unverified and the asset-disposal funding source exhausted — so a demand air-pocket before the Ono/Gama capacity fills would bite the cash statement faster than the glass clock ever could. And **CoWoP is a third mechanism the thesis file does not currently model at all.**
