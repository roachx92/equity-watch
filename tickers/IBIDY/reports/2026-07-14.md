## Executive Summary — Investment Thesis

Ibiden is the world's leading maker of the highest-complexity organic ABF FC-BGA package substrates — the multi-layer organic base that sits underneath high-end CPUs and, increasingly, AI GPUs and accelerators. It has been structurally transformed from an Intel-dependent, PC-cycle industrial into the purest listed pick-and-shovels play on AI accelerator packaging. In the year to March 2026 the customer base inverted: **NVIDIA became the #1 customer at 29.4% of company revenue (¥122.5B), overtaking Intel at 18.1% (¥75.3B)** — a fact disclosed in the primary securities report, not inferred. The stock has risen ~474% over twelve months on this shift, then pulled back ~35% from its ¥27,480 peak into mid-July 2026.

**This is not a survival story.** Ibiden is a real, cash-generative business — FY3/2026 revenue ¥416.2B, operating profit ¥62.0B (+30%), operating cash flow ¥106.4B comfortably covering ¥64.3B of capex, and an equity ratio lifted to 57.3% after paying down ~¥207B of debt. The tension is timing and price, not solvency: the equity trades at ~41x EV/EBITDA and ~87x trailing earnings versus substrate peers at ~9–15x, capitalizing FY3/2028–2029 AI-substrate earnings years before they arrive.

**Three reasons to own — each with its honest counterweight**

- **1) Near-monopoly at the top of the substrate stack.** Illustrative industry estimates put Ibiden at ~35% of high-end AI FC-BGA and 70–80% at the extreme complexity tier (20+ layers, >100mm bodies) where no rival has shown volume GPU-class yield. Counterweight: this is a scope-flip — in the overall substrate market Unimicron is #1 and Ibiden is ~#3; the moat is specifically the top tier, and it is defended by yield know-how, not a patent wall.

- **2) Customers are pre-funding the build.** The ¥500B FY26–28 capex plan (largest in industry history, ~2.5x high-end capacity by 2028) is funded by customer advance payments — reportedly NVIDIA ¥280B + Intel ¥220B. That de-risks the classic substrate over-build trap and hard-wires demand to specific tools. Counterweight: advances on the balance sheet ticked down (¥92.1B→¥81.0B), and a demand air-pocket before the capacity fills would still hurt margins and the multiple.

- **3) A genuine ABF super-cycle with pricing power.** High-end ABF is in a supply-demand gap projected to persist into 2027–2028; Ajinomoto (>95% of ABF film) is raising film prices ~30% from Q3 2026; Ibiden's own profit bridge is ~14:1 price/mix over volume. Counterweight: the film hike is only ~3–6% of substrate cost, and NVIDIA dual-sources Unimicron while the hyperscaler-ASIC wave is heavily contracted to Samsung Electro-Mechanics — Ibiden owns the top, not the whole TAM.

**What has to be true:** the AI accelerator capex cycle keeps compounding (NVIDIA Rubin, with ~+82% ABF content per unit, ramping from Q3 2026; AMD MI450; hyperscaler ASICs); Ibiden fills its ¥500B capacity at margin; and organic substrate holds the high-end until at least ~2028 before glass-core substitution scales. If so, FY3/2027 operating profit guided +45% on +20% revenue shows the operating leverage, and the multiple can hold. If the AI cycle stalls, a rival out-yields the high layer counts, or glass arrives early, a ~41x EV/EBITDA equity that is already +474% de-rates hard.

**Bottom line:** a genuinely wide moat at the top of a structurally tight market, with demand pre-funded by the two most important customers in AI — but after a ~5.7x run and a ~35% pullback, the debate is entirely about price and the 2028–2030 glass clock, not about whether the franchise is real. The quality is not in question; the entry multiple and the substitution timeline are.

## 5. Technological Moat & Competing Technologies

**Core differentiation — high-layer-count yield:** Ibiden's defensible edge is process know-how at 20+ routing layers and >100mm body sizes — the regime where organic substrates fight CTE-mismatch warpage and where no competitor has publicly demonstrated volume GPU-class yield. This is accumulated learning-curve capability, not a single patent, which makes it hard to buy or leapfrog quickly. It is reinforced by (a) qualification lock-in at NVIDIA and Intel (long design-in cycles), and (b) a 3–4 year plant-build-plus-qualification lead time that the ¥500B commitment extends.

**Proprietary vs. commoditizable — the honest split:** genuinely proprietary: top-tier yield, capex scale/lead-time, and design-in incumbency. NOT a moat: mid/low-layer FC-BGA (Unimicron/Nan Ya/Kinsus compete on price), and dependence on Ajinomoto ABF film — an input Ibiden does not own and that a challenger (Hanwha HBF, from 2026) could partly open up. Ajinomoto's ~95% film position is an industry-wide vulnerability, not an Ibiden asset.

**Verdict: DURABLE at the high end through ~2028; a VALUE-MIGRATION (not displacement) risk at the core layer thereafter.** Importantly, glass replaces the substrate CORE (and PTH->TGV), not the ABF build-up layers — those still laminate onto the glass core, so Ibiden's build-up circuit-formation and large-format expertise carries into a glass world (the reason TSMC chose it for CoPoS). The durability question is therefore not 'does ABF disappear' (it does not) but how much value and share Ibiden captures per glass substrate versus today's all-organic stack, where it owns the whole thing. Value migrates toward glass/TGV suppliers (Corning/AGC, LPKF) and possibly toward thin-film-RDL-on-glass processes that favor foundries/OSATs/panel makers — and Ibiden enters glass as a partner/follower, not the near-monopolist it is in organic. Net: erosion of value-capture and share, mitigated by a genuine build-up carry-over and a CoPoS seat — not a clean displacement.

## 6. Secular / Thematic Positioning

Ibiden is levered to two of the strongest structural drivers in technology: AI accelerator compute demand and the packaging/substrate bottleneck that gates it. Every high-end GPU or AI ASIC needs a high-layer FC-BGA substrate, and the content per unit is rising as layer counts climb (3+3 → 11+11 → 13+13) and bodies grow — so Ibiden benefits from both unit growth AND content growth (e.g., NVIDIA Rubin's ~+82% ABF content step-up).

**First- vs. second-derivative:** Ibiden is a first-derivative play on packaging/substrate scarcity but a second-derivative play on AI compute itself — it makes neither the GPU nor the interposer. That is the pick-and-shovels appeal (it wins across whichever accelerator vendor scales) and its ceiling (it captures the substrate slice of the bill of materials, not the chip's value). The Ceramics segment is counter-cyclical ballast tied to the secularly-declining internal-combustion exhaust-filter market — a drag on the blended growth rate, not a driver.

## 8. Risks — Named & Specific

### Pre-Mortem — the single most likely mechanism the thesis breaks

**Named mechanism: the glass-core transition erodes Ibiden's value-capture and share faster than it can defend, around 2028-2030.** Note the nuance (a fair correction to any 'ABF becomes obsolete' framing): glass does NOT displace the ABF build-up layers — they still laminate onto the glass core, so Ibiden's build-up expertise carries over and it is a named CoPoS partner (TSMC-Ibiden-Innolux). Ibiden's ~41x multiple capitalizes years of organic near-monopoly; if that dominance dilutes into a more competitive glass value chain (even without ABF disappearing), both the growth runway and the multiple compress. This is a margin/share-erosion and competitive-repositioning risk, not a clean displacement — less severe than a zero, but the single most important structural question for the out-year thesis.

## 10. Sector Bottleneck / Structural Analysis

The binding constraint in AI accelerator supply is advanced packaging, and within packaging the high-layer organic substrate is one of the tightest links — gated upstream by Ajinomoto's ~95% ABF-film position and by the handful of makers who can yield 20+ layers. Ibiden sits precisely at this chokepoint: it is capacity-constrained, sold out, and pricing up. The supply-demand gap is projected to persist into 2027–2028, and layer-count escalation structurally tightens high-end capacity even as conventional substrate stays balanced.

**Capture mechanism:** Ibiden is not a price-taker exposed to the bottleneck — it is the pick-and-shovels owner of the scarce capability (top-tier yield) and, via customer prepayments, has customers funding its expansion. The migration risk is that the binding constraint itself shifts — from 'who can yield high-layer organic' to 'who can manufacture glass cores' — on a ~2028–2030 horizon, which is exactly the Pre-Mortem. The bottleneck is durable for ~2–3 years and then contested.
