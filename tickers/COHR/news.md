## Thesis context (one-paragraph)
Coherent is a ~$7B-revenue (FY2026 run-rate) **vertically-integrated** photonics and compound-semiconductor maker: it grows its own InP and GaAs wafers, fabricates its own laser die (EMLs, CW DFBs, VCSELs), and assembles them into 800G/1.6T datacom transceivers, 400ZR/800ZR coherent optics, external laser source (ELS) modules, and optical circuit switches. Two segments since 2025-07-01: **Datacenter & Communications** (Q3 FY26 revenue $1,361.7M, **+41% YoY**, 25.5% segment margin — 75% of total and the entire equity story) and **Industrial** ($444.0M, −16% YoY, being pruned by divestiture). Q3 FY26 (qtr ended 3/31/26): revenue **$1,805.6M** (+21% YoY, +27% pro forma), non-GAAP GM 39.6%, non-GAAP EPS $1.41; Q4 guided $1.91–2.05B / $1.52–1.72. **NVIDIA bought 7,788,161 shares at $256.80 on 2026-03-02 for $2.0B**, plus a non-exclusive multi-billion purchase commitment and capacity agreement through 2030 — but wrote the identical $2B cheque to Lumentum the same day. Leverage fell 2.1x → **0.5x** in a year; the entire $2.5B Series B preferred converted and retired; no converts, no ATM, no maturity before 7/1/2029. As of the 2026-07-17 close **$277.60**, market cap ~$54.3B — **−36.9% from the 2026-06-03 high of $440.00** yet still **+50% YTD** and **+229% off the 52-week low**. The debate is **durability and price, not quality or solvency**: earnings power and the balance sheet are intact, but the multiple that compressed (~66x → ~43x forward guided EPS) was built on a co-packaged-optics adoption curve that got pushed out ~a year on 2026-06-09 — while the one metric that genuinely deteriorated (9M FY26 operating cash flow of **$10.1M** vs $503.3M PY, on a $699M inventory build) sits in plain sight and in nobody's narrative.

## Edge (variant view — what the crowd may have wrong)
> **The market has the CPO mechanism backwards, and is worried about the wrong risk.** The 2026-06-09 catalyst (SemiAnalysis, "Powered Down, Lights Off") was a *delay* to co-packaged optics — pushing volume to 2028–29. That is **neutral-to-positive** for Coherent's largest current revenue line, because every quarter CPO does not ramp is a quarter in which 800G/1.6T pluggables remain the only way to move the bits. Coherent supplies **into** CPO regardless — the external laser source module, high-power InP CW lasers, fiber attach units, isolators and TECs — because both NVIDIA and Broadcom deliberately keep lasers **off** the switch package for thermal reasons. And decisively, **EML pluggables, SiPho pluggables and CPO external laser sources all consume InP CW lasers from the same fab**, so the 6-inch InP expansion serves every architecture: Coherent is short the *timing* of the transition but roughly **neutral on the outcome**. So the thing that was sold is roughly neutral-to-positive — while the thing that genuinely deteriorated, **operating cash flow collapsing to $10.1M on a $699M inventory build (disclosed 2026-05-06, *before* the June peak)**, is being traded by nobody in either direction. **The edge is holding both halves at once: the CPO fear is misplaced, *and* the complacency about cash conversion is also misplaced.** If the view is simply "good company, oversold, buy the dip," that is **not** an edge — that is agreeing with a sell-side consensus (~$380–384 avg PT) at a 38% discount to its own target and betting sentiment reverts for no particular reason. Note also what the Edge does **not** claim: the de-rate was not irrational. A terminal-value story moving out a year *should* compress the multiple even when current EPS is untouched, and management's ">$15B CPO TAM" is ~1.5–2x independent estimates (Yole $8.1B by 2030; LightCounting ~$10B by 2030) and is promotional.

## Tripwires (pre-committed exit / re-underwrite triggers)
> Pre-commit to re-underwriting or exiting if ANY of these specific, observable events occurs:
> **(1) THE PRIMARY TRIPWIRE — cash conversion fails to inflect.** On the FY2026 Q4 / full-year report (**expected 2026-08-13**), if full-year operating cash flow comes in **below roughly $300M against ~$700M of capex**, OR **inventory fails to decline from $2,126.8M** (3/31/26), the ramp thesis is not working. Chosen deliberately over a revenue miss because revenue is guided and largely pre-sold, whereas a demand pushout shows up in cash conversion *first*. Decide the action before the print, not after.
> **(2) Concentration converts the story.** If the FY2026 10-K (~mid-Aug 2026) discloses **any single customer above ~20% of revenue**, the name has gone from a diversified merchant supplier to a single-counterparty story and warrants a materially smaller position regardless of fundamentals. (FY2025 baseline: two unnamed customers at **12% and 10%**; FY2024 had only one at 10% — concentration is already rising, and the newest datapoint is ~11 months stale.)
> **(3) Demand digestion at the source.** Any of Microsoft / Alphabet / Meta / Amazon **cutting or materially softening** 2026–2027 capex guidance (late-July 2026 prints and after). Aggregate 2026 guides ~$725B, +77% YoY, all raised in late April — a cut is the fastest route to the bear case and would collide directly with Tripwire #1.
> **(4) The ELS socket commoditizes — the pre-mortem mechanism.** OCP standardization of the ELSFP form factor, OR a **third and fourth vendor qualifying** into the external-laser-source socket (AAOI and Sivers already entering), OR evidence NVIDIA is re-weighting volume toward Lumentum under the identical non-exclusive agreement. This is the only trigger that permanently impairs terminal margin rather than merely compressing the multiple — slow-acting but terminal.
> **(5) Share loss accelerates.** Two consecutive LightCounting/Cignal AI vendor rankings showing Coherent's high-speed datacom share falling, or D&C revenue growth decelerating below ~25% YoY while Innolight/Eoptolink continue compounding faster. (Currently: COHR datacenter +37% YoY vs Innolight +105% and Eoptolink >$1B quarters — already losing share while growing strongly.)
> Any one = the thesis is impaired; decide the action now, not after the drawdown.
