## Q1 2026 — reported 2026-04-30

> **No Tripwire fired.** A weak headline quarter (revenue −32%, Solar collapsed −88%) that the equity — an *option*, not a P&L — mostly shrugged off on the day, then round-tripped to a new ATH weeks later. The two findings that matter are structural, not the loss: **(1)** the cash-generative *funder* under the LIDE option (Solar) eroded faster than the deep-dive assumed, with gross cash down to €5.8m; and **(2)** this print is where management's "first production orders in Q2 2026" framing was set — the expectation the market then crystallized into a "30-June" deadline that **subsequently slipped** (a later, separately-logged event; Tripwire #1's first strike, not yet its second). ### 2. Assessment — what the call does to the thesis

#### (a) The underlying investment thesis

The deep-dive's "what has to be true": *a named glass-substrate maker must place a first multi-tool HVM LIDE order **on LPKF's tool** within the option's decay window (~12–24 months), before sustained burn forces a dilutive raise on the thin float.* Against that conjunction:

- **Leg 1 — LIDE is the qualifying standard:** *modestly supported.* A "first capacity-expansion" LIDE order in hand and "more tangible" supply chains are real, small steps — but it is not HVM, it is unquantified, and Philoptics is co-qualified at the same lines.
- **Leg 2 — the catalyst calendar produces the first HVM order:** *left untested at Q1, then weakened after.* The print delivered no HVM order and set a "Q2 2026" expectation that **subsequently slipped past 30 June** — so with hindsight this leg is weaker than the print alone showed. **Discount management's forward framing by the Guidance track record: two-for-two misses** (FY2025 guide missed; Q2-order timing slipped). A thesis leg leaning on this management's dates is not the same leg it was before the table existed.
- **Leg 3 — the funders hold until the option pays:** *weakened.* Solar collapsed to €1.3m and swung to a −€1.6m EBIT loss; the cross-subsidy under LIDE is eroding *faster* than the deep-dive assumed, and gross cash is €5.8m. The "before sustained burn forces a raise" clause is under more pressure than at deep-dive time.

**Net: thesis weakened, not broken.** The structural (2027 ecosystem) story firmed; the *company-specific execution and funding* legs softened.

#### (b) Re-rating drivers (four types, kept separate)

- **Fundamental (a named HVM order removes the credibility discount):** **not delivered** — the small capacity-expansion order is not the discount-removing event; the print instead created the Q2 expectation that later failed. The heaviest bucket, moved *negatively* on timing.
- **Structural (2027 glass-core HVM timeline):** **corroborated** by peers (SEMCO 2027, GlaSSEM 2H'27, Absolics racing) — but note the deck's *own* roadmap places glass **ramp-up 2027–2028 and high-volume 2029–2030**, i.e. 2027 is ramp *start*, not volume. Intact, with a timing nuance to watch against Tripwire #4.
- **Sentiment/technical:** the −8% dip and snap-back to ATH, plus a short being built — **transient, mean-reverting**; not a re-rate. This bucket did the visible work on the day and none of it stuck.
- **Macro:** not a driver this quarter.

**A re-rate is multiple expansion — and none happened.** The price moved and reverted through a roughly unchanged option premium. The bucket the print weighted most was **fundamental (negative on timing)**; the sentiment bucket was the noise.

#### (c) Risks

- **Funder erosion — ESCALATED.** Solar €1.3m (−88%), swung to −€1.6m EBIT; Welding still loss-making. The cash-generative base under the option is thinner and shrinking faster than the deep-dive modeled.
- **Dilution (Tripwire #3) — moved closer, not tripped.** Gross cash €5.8m, net cash ≈ −€1.5m, FCF −€7.6m/qtr. Runway leans on the syndicated facility (term to 2028); no raise, but the distance to needing one shortened.
- **Rival/dual-sourcing (Tripwire #2) — amber.** Absolics dual-TGV-sourcing confirmed and Philoptics co-qualified at the two most advanced lines; no *named displacement* of LIDE, so not fired — but this is exactly the erosion pattern the tripwire watches.
- **The gating risk (no HVM order) — unchanged and outside LPKF's control.**

**Pre-Mortem update.** The deep-dive's single most-likely path to permanent impairment is *the glass ramp arriving on a rival's tool, forcing a dilutive raise on a thin float at a depressed price.* This print moved the **dilution arm modestly closer** (funder gone, cash €5.8m) and left the **rival-socket arm unchanged** (no named displacement). The mechanism is intact and slightly nearer on the funding axis.

### 3. Final thoughts — what to watch going forward

**1. The read in one line.** After Q1, LPKF is the same event-gated option it was — but with a *thinner funding cushion and a dented credibility clock*: the quarter delivered a weak P&L, a small non-HVM order, and a Q2 promise that then slipped, while Solar (the funder) collapsed and gross cash fell to €5.8m.
