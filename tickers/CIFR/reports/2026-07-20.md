## Executive summary — investment thesis

**What it is, and what the debate actually is.** Cipher Digital (renamed from Cipher Mining, effective 2026-02-20 `[P]`) is a bitcoin miner that has converted itself into an AI/HPC datacenter developer-lessor in ERCOT. It has signed three long-dated leases totalling **~$11.3B across 495 contracted IT MW** — two to Amazon Data Services (Black Pearl, Stingray) and one to the neocloud Fluidstack (Barber Lake, with a Google backstop). It has recognized **$0** of that revenue. Its only operating segment is still Bitcoin Mining `[P]`.

The debate is **not** whether the backlog is real, and **not** whether the counterparties are creditworthy. Amazon is investment grade and guarantees at parent level. The debate is **conversion**, and this report's central finding is that conversion risk is far more concentrated on a single date than the consensus framing admits.

**The three hard facts that anchor the snapshot.**

1. **Every credit enhancement in the structure is inoperative until rent commences.** FY25 10-K, verbatim: *"Our tenants' guarantees and other backstop arrangements under our HPC leases **will only be effective after rent commencement** under such leases and are subject to certain limitations, like event of default triggers or caps."* The risk factor continues that if completion is delayed, tenants may terminate — and *"Such termination events **would not trigger the tenants' guarantee either**."* `[P]` The AWS parent guarantee and the Google backstop — the entire counterparty-quality argument — do not exist yet.
2. **The Google warrant carries an uncapped ratchet keyed to that same date.** 24,178,576 warrants at $0.01, issued 2025-09-24 as consideration for the Fluidstack backstop. The exercise window opens **on the Barber Lake rent-commencement date**. If, on the business day before, the warrant shares are worth **less than $430.0M**, Cipher must issue **additional shares and/or pay cash** for the shortfall `[P]`. That implies a reference price of **~$17.79** — and CIFR closed **$17.56 on 7/17**, with an intraday low of **$16.13**. The disclosed 5.4% is a **floor** on dilution, not a ceiling.
3. **Operating cash flow is an artifact.** Reported OCF was **+$91.5M**, but working capital contributed **+$93.4M** ($83.3M liabilities build + $10.1M asset release), so **OCF ex-working-capital was −$1.9M**. Further: **all** Q1 revenue was non-cash — the add-back *"Non-cash consideration received for services (34,838)"* equals total revenue of $34,838k exactly, with bitcoin sale proceeds ($66.8M) sitting in *investing* — and **cash paid for interest was $0**, because first coupons fall due May/Aug 2026 on $5.2B of debt `[P]`. True capex was ~$685M; FCF ≈ **−$462.5M**.

**Three reasons to own — each with its honest counterweight.**

- **~55% of the backlog sits behind an Amazon.com parent guarantee.** Black Pearl (~$5.5B, 216 IT MW, 15-yr) and Stingray (~$2.0B, 70 IT MW, 15-yr) are both Amazon Data Services, guaranteed by Amazon.com — not the AWS operating subsidiary — and on Stingray Amazon additionally **covers construction cost overruns above a specified cap** `[P/T]`. That is a genuinely favourable term and the strongest part of the structure. **Counterweight:** it is inoperative until commencement (fact 1), and the two contract values ($5.5B, $3.8B) **could not be sourced to any filed or furnished SEC document** — only Stingray's ~$2.0B is primary `[P]`.
- **The project financing is real, priced, and closed.** $2.0B at **6.125%** (Black Pearl, at par), $810M at **6.000%** (Stingray, at 99.750%), $1.733B at 7.125% (Cipher Compute), all non-recourse at project level, with amortization deferred until the respective facility completes `[P]`. Roughly $3.53B of restricted construction escrow is already funded. **Counterweight:** that escrow is ring-fenced to the projects and unavailable to the parent, so the honest net-debt figure depends entirely on which cash you count — **$4,490M against unrestricted cash, $959M against total cash.** Stockholders' equity is **$714.2M** against $5.65B of liabilities.
- **The first proof point is weeks away.** The Black Pearl AWS lease reaches first capacity delivery in **July 2026**, with rent commencing the following month `[T]`; Barber Lake Phase I targets **2026-09-30** `[P]`. Management stated on 2026-06-08 that both are *"on schedule and tracking towards delivery of the initial capacity in September 2026"* `[P]`. **Counterweight:** that statement is six weeks old, and an **ERCOT Batch Zero classification lands 2026-08-07** that no party can pre-empt — no classification exists yet for any of the three campuses.

**What has to be true.** All of: (a) Barber Lake and Black Pearl energize and commence on the September–October 2026 window; (b) ERCOT classifies all three sites as studied/committed rather than excluded on 8/07; (c) Fluidstack remains able to pay ~$380M/yr against its own ~$27B aggregate lease book across four landlords; and (d) the share price sits above ~$17.79 when the Barber Lake window opens, or the ratchet converts the delay into permanent dilution. These are conjunctive **and they are keyed to the same calendar quarter.**

**Bottom line.** The market has already re-specified this name: CIFR fell **39.8% from its 6/18 close to 7/17 while Bitcoin *rose* from ~$58K to ~$65K**, then rallied **+17.0% today with Bitcoin flat**. It is an AI-datacenter lessor with a residual bitcoin option, and the AI factor dominates roughly 5:1. The July de-rate was not about CIFR — it was Meta Compute converting the sector's core premise, compute scarcity, into a supply question. The differentiated view is not "is the backlog real." It is that **the backlog's credit enhancements, its dilution ratchet, its debt amortization, and its regulatory dependency all switch on at one unreached date** — which makes this a far more binary September/October event than a $11.3B contracted book implies. Sixteen analysts, **zero holds and zero sells**, average target $32.53 — and Morgan Stanley cut its target today, on a +17% tape.

## 1. Business model & role in the value chain

Cipher owns land, power interconnection and shells in Texas, builds datacenters on them, and leases the finished capacity to hyperscalers and neoclouds under long-dated contracts. Bitcoin mining, formerly the whole company, is now the transitional cash engine being run down.

| Segment | Q1'26 revenue | YoY | Operating result | Read |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bitcoin Mining** (only reported segment) | **$34,838k** | **−28.8%** (from $48,959k) | **$(67,825)k** vs $(14,834)k | Declining by design; loss more than quadrupled YoY |
| **HPC hosting** | **$0** | n/a | n/a | *"These leases have not yet commenced and no revenue has been recognized as of March 31, 2026"* `[P]` |
| **Consolidated** | **$34,838k** | −28.8% | $(67,825)k | 100% of revenue is the segment being wound down |

Cipher operates **one** segment for reporting purposes and states it will add an HPC segment *"when leases for those data centers commence operations"* `[P]`. That is the cleanest possible statement of where this company actually is.

**Position in the value chain — what Cipher is *not*.** It is not a chip maker, not a GPU owner, and **not a cloud operator**. This distinction is the core of the model and separates it from IREN and CoreWeave: Cipher sells **space, power and cooling** under triple-net-style leases and takes no GPU obsolescence risk, no utilization risk, and no compute-pricing risk — but correspondingly captures **none** of the compute-layer margin. Its economics are a spread between the cost of energized power and contracted rent, levered with project debt. The equity story is entirely the HPC segment that does not yet exist; mining is ballast with a stated expiry.

**One structural correction to the prevailing framing.** The portfolio is not uniformly triple-net. Black Pearl and Stingray (both AWS) are true NNN; **Barber Lake is a *modified gross* lease** `[T]` — meaning Cipher retains opex exposure there. Roughly **40% of contracted IT MW sits on the weaker lease structure, and it is the same 40% leased to the weaker credit.** Those two facts compound rather than offset.

## 4. Technological moat & competing approaches

Cipher's differentiation is not technology — it is **energized power with completed interconnection studies in a market where demand exceeds deliverable supply by ~5×.** The competing "technologies" here are competing *business models* for supplying AI compute.

| Approach | How it works | Champions | Trade-off vs. Cipher's model |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Miner-pivot landlord** (Cipher's model) | Converts ex-mining sites with existing interconnection into shells; 10–20yr NNN leases | CIFR, WULF, HUT, CLSK, GLXY, CORZ | **Baseline.** Fastest path to energized power; ~84–93% modeled mature EBITDA margins; no GPU obsolescence. But high cost of capital, single-tenant concentration, and a margin *ceiling* fixed by the escalator (~3%/yr) — captures none of the compute-layer upside |
| **Owner-operator neocloud** | Buys GPUs, sells GPU-hours | IREN, CoreWeave, Nebius, Fluidstack | Far higher revenue per MW and captures scarcity rent — but 4–6yr GPU depreciation is real residual risk, and this is precisely the model Meta Compute attacks |
| **Incumbent wholesale colo** | Traditional datacenter development | QTS, Vantage, Switch, Aligned | Cheapest capital (infra funds), deepest operating track record — but **no legacy ERCOT interconnection position; they queue behind everyone.** Speed to energization is where miners structurally win |
| **Hyperscaler self-build** | Build it yourself | AWS, Google, Microsoft, Meta | Cheapest capital in the world; hyperscalers are ~44% of global capacity today → ~61% by 2030 `[T]`. Gated by the *same* power queue — which is precisely why they lease from Cipher at all |
| **Hyperscaler capacity resale** | Sell surplus compute | **Meta Compute (2026-07-01)** | The newest and least-analyzed. Doesn't create shells — but converts a capacity-short buyer into a capacity-long seller. See §5 |

**Where the industry is converging:** on **hyperscaler-underwritten, third-party-built, triple-net-leased shells** — the shape of every recent mega-deal (Hut 8 $19.6B, WULF $19B, CIFR $11.3B). The binding constraint is **power, not capital and not GPUs.**

**Defensibility assessment — and the honest verdict.** What is genuinely defensible: energized ERCOT power with completed interconnection studies; ~66% of backlog behind an Amazon.com parent guarantee; demonstrated project-debt access at 6.000–6.125%. What is not: the shell itself is undifferentiated (land + transformer order + queue slot), tenant relationships are non-exclusive (AWS leases from everyone), and the credit enhancement on the weakest lease is **rented from Google rather than earned**.

**Verdict: emerging, not durable.** Five other public companies executed this identical playbook, two of them at larger scale within the last three weeks. **A moat that four competitors replicated in twelve months is a timing advantage, not a moat.**

## 6. Secular / thematic positioning

Cipher is **first-derivative on power scarcity** — its ERCOT position is precisely the asset the entire sector is bidding for — and **second-derivative on AI adoption**, since it sells capacity to those who sell intelligence. ## 8. Risks

**Tenant concentration.** Three campuses, effectively **two credits**. ~66% of backlog (~$7.5B) sits behind Amazon; ~34% (~$3.8B) behind Fluidstack. The 10-K names both explicitly: *"our data centers are single-tenant properties… If we were to lose one or more of our customers, including by defaulting on one of our leases with Amazon or Fluidstack/Google"* `[P]`. The only revenue-concentration disclosure that exists in the FY25 10-K relates to **Foundry USA Pool** — a *mining* counterparty, not HPC.

**The guarantee-timing risk — the most important risk factor in the document.** Guarantees and backstops are *"only effective after rent commencement"* and are *"subject to certain limitations, like event of default triggers or caps."* If completion is delayed, tenants may terminate — and **such termination would not trigger the guarantee either** `[P]`. Counterparty quality is therefore **conditional on execution**, not a hedge against it.

**Fluidstack's capacity to pay, quantified.** ~$3.8B over 10 years ≈ **~$380M/yr** to Cipher alone, against Fluidstack's last hard revenue datapoint of **~$180M ARR (Dec 2024)** — CIFR rent alone is ~2.1× its entire company revenue at that mark. More importantly, Fluidstack's **aggregate lease book across CIFR, TeraWulf Lake Mariner, the Abernathy JV and Hut 8 is ~$27B**, against **~$6.2B of Google backstop — roughly 23% coverage.** Cipher is not exposed to Fluidstack alone; it is exposed to Fluidstack's entire commitment stack, in which Cipher is one of four landlords competing for the same cash. Fluidstack is private, unrated, runs **95% loan-to-cost** financing, carries extreme Anthropic customer concentration, and **withdrew from a major French datacenter project** in 2026 `[T]`.

**The backstop protects lenders, not equity.** The $1.73B is sized *"in support of project-related debt"* and equals **46% of Barber Lake's $3.8B initial term** (~25% with extensions). In a Fluidstack default the likely sequence is: backstop absorbs the debt-service portion, and **Cipher is left with an empty 207MW hall, an unrecovered equity stub, and re-tenanting risk on ~$2.1B of residual contracted revenue.**

**The warrant ratchet.** If the 24,178,576 warrant shares are worth <$430.0M when the exercise window opens at Barber Lake commencement, Cipher must issue **additional shares and/or pay cash** for the shortfall `[P]`. Reference price ~$17.79. The warrant is carried as a **liability at $481,550k** — meaning a *rising* share price *increases* the liability and *widens* reported net loss.

**Regulatory/power.** ERCOT Batch Zero classification on **2026-08-07** is unobtainable today and could exclude a site. The 10-K names ERCOT, **Oncor** and AEP as specific dependencies `[P]`.

**Governance and scale.** **66 full-time employees** against a multi-GW, $11B development program. Auditor is **CBIZ CPAs P.C.**, a mid-tier firm, for a company carrying ~$6B of debt. Say-on-pay drew **16.5% against** `[P]`.

**Insurance.** Property, builders' risk, general liability and business interruption are disclosed in scope only — **no limits, deductibles, carriers, or delay-in-startup coverage are quantified**, and the company caveats that *"certain risks may be uninsured or underinsured"* `[P]`. Given lease termination rights tied to delivery milestones, the absence of any disclosed DSU cover is a genuine unquantified exposure.

**Related-party:** essentially nil and clean — no related-party receivables at 3/31/26 `[P]`.

### The Pre-Mortem — the single most likely path to permanent capital impairment

**Not a demand failure and not a counterparty default.** The named mechanism is a **timing cascade**:

ERCOT's 8/07 classification delays or excludes one campus, or construction slips a quarter. Because guarantees are inoperative until commencement, the delay is *unprotected* — and if it triggers a tenant termination right, the guarantee never vests at all. The equity falls on the news. The Barber Lake warrant window then opens with the shares below ~$17.79, forcing **additional issuance or cash** at the worst possible moment. The revolver, capped at $50M and conditioned on a $3B market cap, is unavailable exactly then. Cipher must fund remaining construction from an ATM into weakness — which is Tripwire #2 — while $6.0B of debt begins amortizing and cash interest starts for the first time. **The backlog survives entirely intact on paper while the equity is permanently diluted underneath it.**

The tell that this path is live is not any single tripwire. It is that **the guarantee vesting date, the ratchet date, the amortization start and the ERCOT gate are all the same quarter.**

## 12. Asymmetry verdict

**A genuine asymmetry, but a leveraged directional one — not a low-floor/high-ceiling bet.**

An option whose exercise conditions include "and the stock must be above ~$17.79 on an unknown date in Q3/Q4 2026" is not a clean asymmetric bet; it is a bet on a narrow window. **Sizing should reflect that this is single-window-dependent.**

## 18. Final thoughts & conclusion

Cipher Digital has done the hard part of the pivot: it secured power, signed $11.3B of long-dated leases with genuinely strong counterparties on two of three campuses, and financed the builds at competitive spreads without recourse to the parent. The market is not disputing any of that. What it repriced in July was **when the money arrives and what it is worth when it does** — and Meta Compute made the second question harder by turning the sector's scarcity premise into a supply question.

Every thread in this report converges on one place. The **business model** is a spread between energized power and contracted rent, unrealized. The **backlog** is real but its two largest values are unverifiable in filed documents. The **moat** is a queue position that Batch Zero is about to re-sort administratively. The **financials** show negative cash flow ex-working-capital, zero cash interest paid on $6.0B of debt, and −$462.5M of quarterly FCF. The **guarantees** that justify the counterparty-quality argument **do not exist until rent commences**. And the **Google warrant** converts a delay into permanent dilution automatically, on that same date, at a reference price the stock traded below three sessions ago.

### Pre-investment checklist

- **Your edge — what do you believe that the crowd doesn't?** Sixteen analysts, **zero holds and zero sells**, average target $32.53, all underwriting the same fact: an $11.3B contracted book with investment-grade counterparties. That is consensus, not edge. The variant view is that **counterparty quality and execution risk are not two independent legs of this thesis — they are the same leg.** The 10-K says the guarantees *"will only be effective after rent commencement,"* and that a delay-driven termination *"would not trigger the tenants' guarantee either."* So the credit quality everyone is paying for is **contingent on the exact milestone that constitutes the risk** — it protects the thesis only in the states of the world where the thesis was already fine. Layer on a second, almost entirely unpriced mechanism: the Google warrant's **$430.0M ratchet**, vesting at Barber Lake commencement, which forces additional shares or cash if the stock is below ~**$17.79** on that date — a price it printed as recently as 7/17.
